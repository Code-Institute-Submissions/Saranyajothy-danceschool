About Guru

Saranya was born in Tamilnadu where Bharatanatyam originated and nurtured to rest of the world.

Saranya did her schooling in Nagercoil, where her budding interest in dancing developed into a passion. Saranya completed integrated bachelor’s degree in fine arts (2005-2010) with a first class honours and a gold medal from Kalai kaveri College of fine arts, affiliated to Bharathidasan University, Tamilnadu.

Saranya started her career with Kalai kaviri College of fine arts as a part-time lecturer for certificate students and simultaneously Saranya also completed a Master’s Degree in Bharatanatyam with yet another first class honours and a gold medal. Saranya holds a certified degree in folk dance in Kalari Panpaatu Maiyam, Nagercoil and a diploma degree in yoga from Centre for Yoga and spiritual education, Aliyar, Tamilnadu. Over the years, Saranya’s Guru in Bharatanatyam is Srimathi. Ogeswari Shanmuganathan, a student of kalashethra and Retired Head of Department for dance in Kalai Kaviri College of fine arts.

Saranya is a trained dancer in Mohiniyattam, a classical dance of Kerala and Kuchipudi, a classical dance from Telugana and Andrapradesh, India. Saranya gained a diploma in Dance Teacher Training with first class honours from the Tamil University, Thanjavur, Tamil Nadu. While taking a career as a Bharatanatyam Teacher and as a part time lecturer from 2007 to 2012 for students enrolled for Diploma in Bharatanatyam in Kalai Kaviri College of fine arts.

Saranya was one of the main dancers of Kalai Kaveri dance troupe from 2006 to 2012 and being an eminent member of Kalai Kaviri College of fine arts. Saranya has performed more than 500 stage performances across and till date India, Germany, Netherlands, Srilanka and Singapore. As a motivating teacher Saranya trained 28 students in their Diploma in Bharatanatyam and successfully guided them to perform their Arangetrams. Saranya has also done creative dance therapy on education in Kalai Kaveri College of fine arts. Saranya worked as a Dance teacher in DISHA - A life school in Pollachi, Tamil Nadu and then moved to Ireland in 2014. Saranya has a huge devotion and passion in Bharatanatyam.

In Ireland, Saranya founded Anugraha Bharatanatyam School with an aim to carry over the age old tradition to next generation Indian diaspora in Ireland and for global audiences. Saranya has provided various guest lectures for Master of Arts in Ethnochoreography students at University of Limerick, Ireland. Saranya and her students have performed Bharatanatyam and a few fusion dances at various events in Ireland.
